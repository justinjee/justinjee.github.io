Letter in response to “Music meets science”


My first piano lesson in college, my teacher said, “You play like a scientist.” It was not a compliment. I think about that whenever a scientist says that research is an art (I‘ve yet to hear an artist do likewise with science). Recently, Detournay (Nature 499, 245:2013) discussed similarities between scientific research and music composition. They both undergo phases of “onset, development, refinement, and exposition.” But so do hedge fund management and tomato farming.

On a deeper level, the one thing that binds music and science is the notion that sincere, personal investment in one’s work is good . Many associate this idea with artists but not scientists, so it is understandable that some scientists label themselves “artists” and point to the similarities. But science and art are not the same. Science is the pursuit of truth, and it lives on one emotion: the thrill of discovery. Music is about evoking and savoring the emotions themselves.


Justin Jee
